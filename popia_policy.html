Rise Batho Foundation POPIA Policy

Effective Date: 26 June 2024

The Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA) aims to protect your personal information and regulates how your personal information is processed. Rise Batho Foundation is committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring that your personal information is collected and used properly, lawfully, and transparently.

This policy outlines how Rise Batho Foundation collects, uses, discloses, and protects your personal information in compliance with POPIA.

1. What Personal Information Do We Collect?

We collect personal information directly from you when you register as a youth, engage with our services, or communicate with us. The types of personal information we may collect include, but are not limited to:

  • Identity Information: Full names, date of birth, gender, race (optional).
  • Contact Information: Mobile number, email address, street address, suburb, city/town, province, postal code.
  • Background Information: Education level, current schooling status, employment status, whether actively looking for work, qualifications/certificates.
  • Personal Narratives: Skills/interests, types of help needed, goals/challenges.
  • Referral Information: How you heard about Rise Batho Foundation.
  • Consent Information: Your acceptance of this POPIA policy, date of form completion.
  • Health Information (Optional): Indication of disability status.

2. How Do We Use Your Personal Information?

Your personal information is collected for specific, explicitly defined, and lawful purposes related to our mission of empowering youth. These purposes include:

  • To process and manage your registration as a beneficiary of our foundation.
  • To understand your specific needs, challenges, and aspirations.
  • To match you with appropriate work, education, mentorship, or other support opportunities.
  • To communicate with you regarding programs, workshops, resources, and events.
  • To build a supportive community network.
  • For internal record-keeping, reporting, and statistical analysis (always anonymized where possible).
  • To comply with legal and regulatory requirements.

We will not use your personal information for any other purpose without your express consent.

3. Disclosure of Personal Information

Rise Batho Foundation will not sell, rent, or lease your personal information to third parties. We may disclose your personal information only under the following circumstances:

  • With Your Consent: When you have given us explicit consent to share your information for a specific purpose (e.g., sharing your profile with a potential employer or educational institution).
  • To Service Providers: To trusted third-party service providers who assist us in operating our foundation and providing services (e.g., database hosting, communication platforms), under strict confidentiality agreements.
  • Legal Compliance: When required by law or a court order, or to protect our rights, property, or safety.

4. Security of Your Personal Information

We are committed to ensuring that your personal information is secure. We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to prevent unauthorised access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction of your personal information, including:

  • Using secure database hosting (Supabase).
  • Implementing Row Level Security (RLS) on our database.
  • Limiting access to personal information to authorized personnel only.
  • Regularly reviewing our security practices.

5. Your Rights Under POPIA

As a data subject, you have the right to:

  • Right to Access: Request access to your personal information that we hold.
  • Right to Rectify: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal information.
  • Right to Object: Object to the processing of your personal information under certain circumstances.
  • Right to Erasure (Deletion): Request the deletion or destruction of your personal information (subject to legal obligations).
  • Right to Lodge a Complaint: Lodge a complaint with the Information Regulator if you believe your rights under POPIA have been infringed.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details provided below.

6. Retention of Personal Information

We will retain your personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by law.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this POPIA policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. We will notify you of any significant changes by posting the updated policy on our website.
